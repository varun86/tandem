# Character Sheets

## Standard Character Archetypes

### A - Protagonist

The main character the audience follows. Usually:

- Facing a problem or desire
- Makes choices that drive the story
- Has a clear want and obstacle

**Voice traits:**

- Speaks in full sentences
- Often reactive at first, then proactive
- Emotional responses are visible

### B - Catalyst/Antagonist

The character who creates change:

- Opposes or challenges A
- Or enables A's transformation
- Often mysterious or intriguing

**Voice traits:**

- Shorter, punchier lines
- Often speaks in fragments
- Creates more questions than answers

### C - Supporting Character

Secondary characters who:

- Provide information
- Create complications
- Reflect themes

**Voice traits:**

- Functional dialogue
- Appears briefly
- Serves plot purpose

## Building Characters

### For Each Character, Define:

1. **What they want**: Surface desire
2. **What they need**: Internal need
3. **What's in their way**: Obstacle
4. **How they speak**: Verbal quirks
5. **What they do physically**: Physical traits

---

## Example Character Profiles

### A - The Professional

- **Want**: Success, approval
- **Need**: Work-life balance, genuine connection
- **Obstacle**: Perfectionism, fear of failure
- **Voice**: Polished, careful word choice
- **Action**: Checks phone constantly, takes notes

### B - The Free Spirit

- **Want**: Freedom, adventure
- **Need**: Belonging, stability
- **Obstacle**: Responsibility, fear of missing out
- **Voice**: Casual, uses slang, short responses
- **Action**: Always moving, looks out windows

---

## Character Consistency Rules

1. Names stay consistent throughout episode
2. Voice patterns don't change mid-episode
3. Reactions match established personality
4. Physical actions align with character
5. Growth is earned through events
